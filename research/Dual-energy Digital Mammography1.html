Dual-energy Digital Mammography

Mammography is currently the gold standard for breast cancer screening. The determination of masses and calcifications in mammograms is very important in the early diagnosis of breast cancer. However, the visualization of calcifications could be obscured in mammograms because of overlapping of tissue structures .Dual-Energy Digital Mammography (DEDM) is considered as a prospective technique to improve the calcification detectability.

We are working on the physical principle of DEDM, discover the disadvantages of existing algorithm of DEDM and propose new methods: scatter correction method with no extra dose using the correlation of projection data; noise reduction technique. A new calculation frame based on DEDM has been proposed. This frame would generate accurate micro-calcification image using dual-energy calculation and synthetic image similar to conventional mammography image using multi-scale decomposition and synthesis.

The goal of this research is to develop a new based on DEDM that can be applied to the existing digital mammography system without hardware modification and improve the diagnosis of breast cancer.
